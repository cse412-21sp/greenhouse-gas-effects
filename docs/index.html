Consequences - Policy

This section will look at the various ways in which countries have attempted to decrease their environmental impact.

First we will look at an index done by OCED to measure how stringent a country’s environmental policies are as a whole. The environmental policy index is an index from 0 (least stringent) to 6 (most stringent) based on 14 environmental policy ratings focusing on climate and air pollution.

This visualization shows many of the countries with the highest environmental policy index are in Europe, with Canada in the range with Europe as well. Additionally, it is interesting that much of the data is missing for Central and South America, Africa, and Central Asia.

Next we will look at what countries use taxes related to environmental effects using environmental taxes as a percent of GDP.

Here we find that most countries have very small environmental tax rates, with most countries outside of Europe in the 1-2% rate, and much of Europe in the 2-3% rate. Interesting outliers include Mauritania with an environmental tax rate above 5% of GDP, and Mexico with an environmental tax rate of -.2% of GDP.

It combines years of life lost and years of healthy life lost due to sickness or disability. Below is a map of the average DALYs per 1000 residents averaging from 1990-2019.

Below is a map that shows the DALYs per 1000 residents from 1990-2019. Note that some years do not have data and appear gray.
